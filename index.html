Hunter's Scripps in DC Reflection:

During my semester of living, learning, and interning at the National Fisheries Institute in the heart of the nation’s capital, I witnessed protests and politics in action. I listened to award- winning journalists, photojournalists, and visual communicators paint pictures and scenes of what they covered with descriptive words, vivid images, and amazing illustrations. I watched members of Congress try to play baseball on the Nat's baseball diamond while lobbyists pitched their proposals in the stands and just off the playing field. I visited historic monuments and buildings and walked, jogged, and biked on the paths/walkways of the United States Capitol—the same ones traveled by many historical trailblazers. I toured famous museums of art, history, science, and culture. I attended concerts and shows at the 930 club, Kennedy Center, Fords Theatre, Sydney Harmon Hall, and Warner Theatre. I explored the wild side of Washington and Baltimore with a trip to living treasure-the Washington Zoo and the Baltimore Aquarium. I took Ubers, subways, and bikes around the city and to the National Archives. I had lunch and dinner at some of amazing restaurants, and I ate fake fish and realized some things should remain as plants. During my free time, I cheered on the University of Cincinnati football team to victory over Navy and spurred on the Washington Nationals. I kayaked. I rock climbed. I met interesting Ohio University alumni and made some new Bobcat friends. Most importantly, I learned that living, learning, and working in the heart of the nation’s capital is something that I would one day like to do.
